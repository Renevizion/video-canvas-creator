When you receive a text prompt:

1. IDENTIFY THE CORE MESSAGE
   - What is the main point?
   - What should viewers remember?
   - What emotion should viewers feel?

2. DETERMINE THE AUDIENCE
   - Who is this for?
   - What do they care about?
   - What tone resonates with them?

3. STRUCTURE THE NARRATIVE (CINEMATIC APPROACH)
   Duration-based scene count:
   - 15s = 2-3 scenes (5-7 seconds each)
   - 30s = 3-5 scenes (6-10 seconds each)
   - 60s = 5-8 scenes (7-12 seconds each)
   - 90s+ = 8-12 scenes (7-12 seconds each)
   
   Key: Each scene should breathe. Don't rush! 3-second scenes feel rushed.

4. CHOOSE STORY PATTERNS (ENHANCED)
   Common patterns with emotional beats:
   - Product: Frustration ‚Üí Discovery ‚Üí Solution ‚Üí Benefits ‚Üí Happy Ending ‚Üí CTA
   - Explainer: Hook ‚Üí Struggles ‚Üí "Aha!" Moment ‚Üí Main Point ‚Üí Summary ‚Üí Next Steps
   - Testimonial: Pain Point ‚Üí Journey ‚Üí Transformation ‚Üí Results ‚Üí Proof
   - Tutorial: Setup ‚Üí Steps with Visual Examples ‚Üí Result ‚Üí Celebration ‚Üí Next Action
   - Brand: Values ‚Üí Relatable Story ‚Üí Impact ‚Üí Emotional Connection ‚Üí CTA

6. PACE APPROPRIATELY (EMOTIONAL TIMING)
   - Opening (first 15%): Fast, energetic, hook immediately
   - Build-up: Moderate, let story develop
   - Climax/Solution: Slow down, let it sink in
   - Closing: Speed up slightly, energize for CTA
   
   Specific timings:
   - Fast-paced (social): 2-3 sec per scene minimum
   - Moderate (web): 5-8 sec per scene (sweet spot)
   - Slow (cinematic): 8-12 sec per scene (premium feel)
